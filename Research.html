Research

From fish feeding to anole ecomorphology to salamander walking, I am fascinated with how both vertebrates interact with their environment. My research integrates functional morphology, biomechanics, and phylogenetic comparative methods to study patterns of morphological and functional diversification. The techniques that I often use are CT scanning, geometric morphometrics, and biomechanical modelling. Details on my past and present research areas can be viewed below.
