Research

I am a functional morphologist, interesting in the traits that animals have evolved to interact with their environment. My approach is integrative and combines morphological, biomechanical, and phylogenetic methods to study the evolutionary patterns and mechanisms of functional and morphological diversification. The major topics that I am currently studying include the biomechanics and kinematics of salamander locomotion, the form and function of fishes with suction cups, the evolution of fish feeding morphology, and anole ecomorphology.
