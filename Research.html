Research

I am a integrative evolutionary biologist interested in how animals interact with their environment and the kinds of morphological adaptations they have evolved. My approach combines functional morphology, biomechanics, and phylogenetic comparative methods to study evolutionary patterns and the underlying mechanisms of functional and morphological diversification. My research interests are broad but can be generally grouped in four main subcategories: 1) salamander limb biomechanics and kinematics, 2) ecomorphology of fishes with suction-based adhesion, 3) the evolution of specialized feeding morphologies, and 4) mainland anole ecomorphology.
