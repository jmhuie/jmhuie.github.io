About Me

I am a recent graduate from the University of Washington interested in the link between ecology and morphology. My work has mainly focused on the feeding morphology of fishes that have unusual diets, but I am working on additional on-going projects that involve anole ecomorphology and fish kinematics. Currently, I am at Friday Harbor Labs in Adam Summers's lab CT scanning fishes for oVert, the initiative to scan all species of vertebrates.

Rheophilic and herbivorous fishes

Pacus are the herbivorous cousins of piranhas. A few species live only in river rapids and eat primarily riverweed, a local plant also restricted to river rapids. These pacus have more streamlined bodies compared to their relatives.

Scale-feeding fishes

Fishes that eat only the scales and mucous of other fishes are surprisingly diverse. One species has a massive jaw for dislodging scales, while another rams its face it its prey. Both are pedomorphic and retain their juvenile dentition.
