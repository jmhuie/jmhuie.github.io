About Me

I am a first year PhD student at George Washington University in the Fins and Limbs lab. I study vertebrate functional morphology, biomechanics, and macroevolution. For my thesis, I will be focusing on salamander limb biomechanics but my past and current research spans across several taxonomic groups. Most recently, my work has focused on using micro-CT scanning to study fish feeding mechanics and body shape. I am also a huge advocate of natural history collections, for which I have been using to study anole ecomorphology.


You can contact me at: jonathanmhuie [at] gmail.com

Evolution of cleaner fishes

Cleaners feed on the parasites on other fishes. In this study we asked whether cleaners exhibit distinct feeding morphologies. Then we tested whether their degree of morphological specialization has co-evolved with their ecological specialization. In short, it does! Read more here .

Body shape of rheophilic fishes

We investigated whether fishes that feed only on plants found in river rapids have specialized jaw morphologies and/or specialized body shapes. Turns out, a streamlined body shape to living in the rapids is more important than what their jaws look like. Read more here .
